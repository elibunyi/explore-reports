1. Audit Findings

P1 — Critical Findings

P1 Critical
Wrong Geography Headline: "Florida Fiberglass Experts" in Waco TX Account
FINDING: The Fiberglass Pools General ad group contains the headline "Florida Fiberglass Experts" — this is a Waco, TX dealer account.
DATA: This headline is one of 15 in the Fiberglass Pools General RSA. It will serve to Waco-area searchers who will see a Florida reference, immediately destroying credibility and relevance.
RECOMMENDATION: Remove immediately. Replace with a locally relevant headline such as "Waco Fiberglass Experts" (24 chars) or "Central TX Pool Experts" (23 chars).
Wrong-geo headlines are the worst kind of ad copy error — they actively signal to the searcher that this ad is not for them. In a market where trust and locality matter (pool installation), a Florida reference in Texas is an instant disqualifier.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P1 Critical
Headline Typo: "Ger Your Free Estimate" — Missing "t" in "Get"
FINDING: Fiberglass Pools General RSA contains "Ger Your Free Estimate" instead of "Get Your Free Estimate".
DATA: This typo serves to searchers and undermines professionalism. The Fiberglass Pools General ad group drove 84 clicks and 6 conversions — it is a high-traffic ad group where this typo has real visibility.
RECOMMENDATION: Fix to "Get Your Free Estimate" (23 chars).
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P1 Critical
All 6 RSAs Have Empty Display Paths (path1 and path2 Blank)
FINDING: Every RSA across all 6 ad groups has path1="" and path2="". Zero display path usage in the entire account.
DATA: Display paths reinforce relevance and improve CTR. Without them, the display URL shows only the root domain with no keyword context. Across 2,725 impressions, every single one showed a bare URL.
RECOMMENDATION: Add paths to every RSA. Examples: "Fiberglass-Pools" / "Waco" or "Pool-Builder" / "Waco-TX". Each path field max 15 chars.
Display paths are free real estate for relevance signals. Google uses them in quality score calculations and searchers use them as trust signals. Leaving them blank is leaving performance on the table at zero cost.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P1 Critical
ZERO Extensions in Entire Account — No Sitelinks, Callouts, Snippets, or Call
FINDING: The account has zero extensions at any level — no sitelinks, no callouts, no structured snippets, no call extension. Not at customer level, not at campaign level, not at ad group level.
DATA: Extensions directly impact Ad Rank (they are a component of the Ad Rank formula). With 51.1% impression share lost to rank on the Pool Builder campaign, missing extensions are a significant contributor to the rank deficit.
RECOMMENDATION: Add all extensions per Sections 10–13 of this report. This is the single highest-impact change for improving impression share.
Google's Ad Rank formula includes expected impact of extensions. An account with zero extensions is competing with a handicap against competitors who have them. In a small metro where budgets are modest, extensions are the cheapest way to gain competitive advantage.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P1 Critical
ZERO Customer Assets — No Business Name, Logo, or Marketing Images
FINDING: No customer-level assets exist in the account. No business name asset, no logo, no marketing images.
DATA: Customer assets enable rich ad formats and improve visual presence in search results. Without them, ads appear as plain text only.
RECOMMENDATION: Upload: (1) Business name: "Leisure Pools Waco", (2) Leisure Pools logo (square + landscape), (3) 3–5 marketing images of completed pool installations.
HIGH CONFIDENCE

P2 — Moderate Findings

P2 Moderate
No Price Anchor Headline in Any Ad Group
FINDING: Zero pricing headlines with a specific dollar amount across all 6 ad groups. The Cost/Pricing ad group has generic pricing headlines ("Pool Prices Waco", "Fiberglass Pool Cost") but no actual price anchor.
DATA: The Cost/Pricing ad group has 20.5% CTR but 0 conversions from 45 clicks and $232 spend. Without a price anchor, low-budget searchers click through, see the real price, and bounce.
RECOMMENDATION: Add "Pools Starting at $35K" (22 chars) pinned to H2 in every ad group. This pre-qualifies traffic and improves lead quality.
Price anchors are the most effective lead quality filter in pool dealer ads. The Cost/Pricing ad group's 20.5% CTR with 0 conversions is a classic sign of unqualified price shoppers clicking through without knowing the price range.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P2 Moderate
ZERO Audience Segments Configured
FINDING: No In-Market, Life Events, Affinity, Custom Intent, or audience exclusions on either campaign.
DATA: Without audience signals, the account treats all searchers identically. High-intent pool buyers (In-Market for Home Improvement) receive the same bid as casual browsers.
RECOMMENDATION: Add audience layers with bid adjustments per Section 14. Use observation mode — do not restrict targeting.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P2 Moderate
ZERO Device Bid Adjustments — Desktop Severely Underperforming
FINDING: No device bid modifiers set on either campaign.
DATA: Mobile: 251 clicks, 11 conversions, $122.75/conv (4.38% conv rate). Desktop: 28 clicks, 1 conversion, $206.17/conv (3.57% conv rate). Tablet: 1 click, 0 conversions, $9.76 spend.
RECOMMENDATION: Set desktop bid adjustment to -25% and tablet to -30%. Mobile is driving 91.7% of conversions at a significantly lower cost — shift budget accordingly. See Section 15.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
P2 Moderate
ZERO Demographic Bid Adjustments
FINDING: No age or household income bid modifiers on either campaign.
DATA: Pool buyers skew 35–64 and higher household income. Without demographic modifiers, the account bids equally for 18–24 year olds (unlikely buyers) and 45–54 year olds (prime buyers).
RECOMMENDATION: Add HHI and age bid modifiers per Section 16.
HIGH CONFIDENCE
