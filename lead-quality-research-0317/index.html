Section 1: Lead Quality Optimization Fundamentals

The Core Problem

When your only conversion point is a form submission, Google's algorithm optimizes for more form submissions -- not better ones. Experts call this the "feedback loop of doom": the algorithm learns to find cheap, easy form fills (tire-kickers, comparison shoppers, students researching) rather than serious buyers.

Source: Search Engine Journal, "Performance Max for Lead Generation: Advanced Strategies and Pitfalls" (2025)

Section 3: Form-Based Conversion Optimization

The Problem

A basic Name/Email/Phone/Message form captures everyone -- including people who are "just looking," students doing research, competitors scouting, and people who can't afford a $50K pool. Every form fill teaches Google's algorithm to find more people like them.

Multi-Step Form Strategy

Step 1 -- Low Friction (Capture Contact)

  • Name
  • Email
  • Phone

Step 2 -- Qualifying (Filter Intent)

  • "What type of pool are you interested in?" (Fiberglass / Concrete / Not Sure)
  • "What is your approximate budget?" (Under $30K / $30K-$50K / $50K-$75K / $75K-$100K / $100K+)
  • "When are you looking to start?" (1-3 months / 3-6 months / 6-12 months / Just researching)

Step 3 -- Detail (Serious Buyers Only)

  • "Do you own your home?" (Yes / No / Renting)
  • "Brief description of your project" (text area)

Key insight: Users who complete Step 1 have invested effort and are psychologically committed -- they finish Step 3 at much higher rates than if all fields were shown at once. You still capture partial data if they drop off at Step 2.

Price as a Qualifier

In Ad Copy: Include "Starting at $45,000" or "Premium Fiberglass Pools from $40K" in headlines or descriptions.

On Landing Pages: "Fiberglass pool installations typically range from $40,000 to $100,000+ depending on size, features, and site conditions."

This immediately disqualifies price-shoppers looking for $5K above-ground pools. Yes, it reduces total form submissions. That's the point.

Sources: leads4build.com, growform.co, leadgenapp.io

Section 5: Campaign Structure for Lead Quality

Broad Match vs. Exact/Phrase Match

Eli's current strategy of exact and phrase match only is correct. Broad match only works well with (a) strong conversion data feeding Smart Bidding and (b) comprehensive negative keywords. Without CRM feedback, broad match optimizes toward quantity, not quality. When/if offline conversion tracking is implemented, test broad match on one high-volume account.

Recommended Campaign Structure

CampaignPurposeBudget %Bid Strategy
Brand ProtectionDefend dealer/brand name5-10%Manual CPC
High-Intent Pool BuilderFiberglass pool, pool builder, pool quote keywords60-70%Max Conv w/ tCPA (after 30+ conv)
Brand-SpecificLeisure/Aviva/Imagine/Evo + city15-20%Manual CPC or ECPC
Remarketing/RLSARecapture serious researchers5-10%+30-50% bid adjustment

Bid Strategy Progression

  1. Weeks 1-4: Manual CPC -- learn which keywords convert
  2. After 15+ conversions: Enhanced CPC -- slight algorithmic adjustments
  3. After 30+ conversions: Maximize Conversions -- full optimization
  4. With enough data: Maximize Conversions with tCPA target
  5. With conversion values: Maximize Conversion Value with tROAS
Critical rule: Never switch to Smart Bidding before 30 conversions/month per campaign. For low-volume dealer accounts, keep Manual CPC or Enhanced CPC.

Section 6: Performance Max for Pool Dealer Lead Gen

The Honest Assessment

Without offline conversion data, PMax optimizes for form fills, not qualified leads. The algorithm tends to serve ads on cheap Display/YouTube inventory rather than high-intent Search, creating a "feedback loop of doom" that finds the easiest, cheapest conversions -- which are often the lowest quality.

If You Use PMax, Do This:

  1. Disable URL Expansion -- prevent traffic to blog posts and About pages
  2. Add 500+ negative keywords -- use the full 10,000 capacity
  3. Exclude brand terms -- run brand Search campaigns separately
  4. Upload customer match lists -- signal what good customers look like
  5. Use "More qualified" lead form optimization
  6. Monitor placement reports -- exclude gaming sites, children's apps
  7. Disable auto-generated YouTube videos
  8. Run PMax alongside Search, not instead of it
  9. Test your URL in Keyword Planner -- if suggested keywords don't match, PMax will struggle

Demand Gen as an Alternative

Demand Gen campaigns provide more targeting control than PMax. For dealers with strong project photo libraries, Demand Gen may be a better complement to Search -- it supports lookalike audiences, offers more manual control, and is better for top-of-funnel with quality guardrails.

Sources: searchenginejournal.com, seerinteractive.com, dmcockpit.com, boomcycle.com

Section 7: Landing Page Strategies That Affect Lead Quality

The 2-5x Lift: Generic website pages convert at ~2%. Dedicated landing pages matched to specific ad groups convert at 5-10%+ using the same traffic.

Elements That Filter for Quality

  • Price Transparency: "Fiberglass pool installations typically range from $40,000 to $100,000+"
  • Project Gallery with Scope: "32ft Riviera | Houston, TX" -- show what $60K looks like
  • Process Timeline: Design > Site survey > Permitting > Installation > Finishing > Handoff. Signals major project, not impulse purchase.
  • Trust Signals: BBB rating, years in business, pools installed count, manufacturer certifications
  • Qualifying CTA: "Schedule Your Design Consultation" instead of "Get a Free Quote" -- language signals seriousness

Mobile Requirements (60%+ of Searches)

  • Load in under 3 seconds
  • Tappable phone numbers
  • Forms limited to 3-5 visible fields
  • 44x44px minimum button size
  • No blocking pop-ups

Sources: leads4build.com, poolmarketingpros.com, involve.me

Section 10: Additional Strategies

Disable Search Partners

Search Partners consistently produce lower-quality traffic for home services. Google now provides site-level reporting. Check performance and disable for most pool dealer accounts.

Call Tracking Configuration

  • Set minimum call duration to 60-90 seconds before counting as conversion
  • Consider third-party tracking (CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics) for call recording and quality scoring
  • Mark calls as qualified/unqualified and import back as offline conversions if possible

RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads)

Pool purchases have a long research cycle. RLSA lets you bid 30-50% more when someone who already visited the dealer's site searches again. Conversion rates for returning visitors are typically 2-5x higher.

Conversion Value Rules (No CRM Required)

Underutilized feature: Go to Tools > Conversions > Value Rules. Create rules based on location, audience, or device. If Dallas leads are worth more than rural Texas leads, multiply Dallas conversions by 1.5x. Smart Bidding will automatically prioritize Dallas impressions.

Micro-Conversions for Low-Volume Accounts

For dealer accounts under 30 conversions/month: track "Contact page views" or "Form start" as secondary conversions to give Smart Bidding more data points. Only include high-intent micro-conversions.

Sources: searchenginejournal.com, klientboost.com, optmyzr.com, Google Ads Help

Section 11: Enhanced Conversions for Leads (Future Priority)

This is the #1 future priority for lead quality. Every expert and case study in this research pointed to offline conversion tracking as THE solution. The problem is it requires knowing which leads are qualified -- which means dealer feedback.

How It Could Work

  1. Dealer website captures lead via form (name, email, phone)
  2. Google captures a hashed version of the email at form submission
  3. When a lead is later determined qualified, upload that email to Google Ads
  4. Google matches the hashed email to the original click
  5. Smart Bidding learns to find more leads like the qualified ones

A Practical Workaround

  1. Set up Enhanced Conversions for Leads via Google Tag Manager
  2. Ask dealers to periodically share which leads booked consultations
  3. Upload those emails via a simple spreadsheet
  4. Even monthly uploads provide value (Google recommends daily)
  5. Minimum threshold: 10 offline conversions/campaign/month

Sources: measureschool.com, Google Ads Help, ppcmastery.com
