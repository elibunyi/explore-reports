1. Executive Summary

Key Findings

This account is running two campaigns (main Search Campaign + new High Intent campaign) with a combined $60/day budget. The main campaign has 13 conversions over 30 days at $185 CPA. However, the account is structurally weak: a single ad group contains ALL keywords for the main campaign, match types are entirely phrase match (good — no broad), but negative keyword coverage is critically thin with only 4 campaign-level negatives plus 25 ad-group-level negatives. Location targeting uses PRESENCE (correct). Search partners are OFF (correct). The High Intent campaign has barely started with only 9 impressions.

Biggest issues: Dangerous negative keyword gap (competitors, DIY, service/repair leaking through), single ad group handles all intent types, no customer-level extensions, Maximize Conversions without a CPC cap on low-volume account, and 40% impression share means 60% of available searches are being missed.

Priority Issues

P1 Negative keyword coverage critically thin — Only 4 campaign-level negatives. Competitors (Scioto Valley, Premier Pools, Marzetti, etc.), DIY, pool service/repair, semi-inground, container pools all leaking through. Estimated $50-80/mo wasted.
P1 Single ad group structure — All keyword intents (brand, generic pool, fiberglass specific, cost/pricing, builder/contractor) crammed into one ad group. Kills Quality Score relevance and ad copy targeting.
P1 No customer-level extensions — Zero sitelinks, zero callouts, zero structured snippets, zero call extensions at the account level. The ads are running naked without any extensions.
P1 Maximize Conversions without CPC cap on low-volume account — 13 conversions/month is below the 15+ threshold where Maximize Conversions works reliably. No CPC ceiling means Google can (and does) spend $10+ per click. Average CPC is $6.69 but individual clicks reaching $10.96.
P1 No income demographic targeting — No income range exclusions. Pool installations are $60K+ purchases. Bottom income tiers are wasting budget on unqualified clicks.
P2 "pool company" keyword has QS 3/10 — Highest-spend keyword ($575 cost, 68 clicks) has terrible quality score. Ad relevance is low because all keywords share one ad group with generic copy.
P2 "inground pool price" keyword has QS 3/10 — Second-highest spend keyword ($339, 70 clicks) also has poor quality score. Cost queries need their own ad group with pricing-focused copy.
P2 High Intent campaign barely serving — 9 impressions in 30 days. Using Maximize Clicks (appropriate for new campaign) but the campaign is not getting volume. May be cannibalizing the main campaign's Columbus-specific terms.
P2 "plunge pool" QS 3/10 — Good keyword (Leisure Pools sells plunge models) but poor QS. Needs its own ad group with plunge-focused copy.
P3 Duplicate keyword coverage across campaigns — "pool builder Columbus" exists in both campaigns, creating internal competition.
P3 Many zero-impression exact match keywords — 15+ exact match keywords in the main campaign with 0 impressions. These are wasting account complexity with no benefit — the phrase match variants cover them.
P3 Ad strength "Good" (6) on all 3 RSAs — Not bad, but not "Excellent." Could be improved with more differentiated headlines and pinning strategy.
