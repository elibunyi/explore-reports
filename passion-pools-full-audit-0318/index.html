Competitor names, pool supply stores, pool service queries, DIY/kit queries, and above-ground pool searches are all matching freely. Estimated wasted spend: $1,153+ over 30 days (~$38/day, 14% of total spend).

CONFIDENCE: HIGH

RECOMMENDATION: Add the full negative keyword list from Section 9 to ALL THREE campaigns immediately. This is the single highest-impact change available.

P1 Single Ad Group Structure — All Campaigns

DATA: Each campaign has exactly one ad group named "Ad group 1" with 25-30 phrase-match keywords. NKY: 339 clicks / 13 conv. Cincinnati: 515 clicks / 12 conv. Dayton: 524 clicks / 20 conv.

REASONING: A single ad group cannot deliver relevant ad copy to diverse search intents. Someone searching "fiberglass pool cost" sees the same ad as someone searching "leisure pools cincinnati." Quality Scores confirm this — "pool companies" has QS=1 in Dayton and QS=3 in Cincinnati/NKY, while branded "leisure pools" terms have QS=10. The mismatch is costing relevance and driving up CPCs.

CONFIDENCE: HIGH

RECOMMENDATION: Restructure each campaign to 3 themed ad groups with tailored RSA copy per Section 5-8.

P1 Maximize Conversion Value on Low-Volume Campaigns

DATA: All 3 campaigns use Maximize Conversion Value. Combined: 45 conversions over 30 days (1.5/day). Per campaign: NKY 13 (~0.43/day), Cincinnati 12 (~0.4/day), Dayton 20 (~0.67/day).

REASONING: Google recommends 30+ conversions/month per campaign for smart bidding to optimize effectively. None of these campaigns meet that threshold individually. Maximize Conversion Value is a value-optimized strategy — even more data-hungry than Maximize Conversions. On low-volume accounts, smart bidding makes erratic decisions, leading to CPC spikes (Dayton avg CPC $7.17, NKY $6.22) and inconsistent performance. Maximize Clicks with a CPC cap would give more control and predictable spend.

CONFIDENCE: HIGH

RECOMMENDATION: Switch all 3 campaigns to Maximize Clicks with CPC caps: NKY $12, Cincinnati $10, Dayton $12. Re-evaluate after 60 days of conversion data accumulates.
