Section 3A — Safety & Certification (6 Questions)

Safety & Certification

Are Integra covers ASTM certified?
Yes. Every Integra automatic pool cover is independently tested and certified to meet or exceed ASTM F1346-91 — the national safety standard for pool covers. This certification confirms the cover can support required weight loads, prevent child entry through perimeter gaps, and drain standing water.
What does ASTM F1346 actually require?
ASTM F1346 mandates three critical tests: (1) a static load test requiring the cover to support 485 lbs without allowing access to the water, (2) perimeter gap requirements preventing a child from slipping between the cover edge and pool coping, and (3) drainage requirements preventing dangerous standing water accumulation on the cover surface.
Can an automatic pool cover replace a pool fence?
In many jurisdictions, yes. An ASTM F1346-certified automatic pool cover qualifies as an approved safety barrier under most building codes. However, requirements vary by state, county, and municipality. We recommend checking our pool cover vs. fence guide or consulting your local building department.
How much weight can the cover hold?
Integra covers are engineered and tested to support 485 lbs in a static load test — enough for two adults and one child to be supported in an emergency scenario. This is the load requirement specified by ASTM F1346.
Is the cover safe for pets?
Yes. The cover is designed to prevent accidental entry for both children and pets. The fabric tension, perimeter gap engineering, and reinforced edges ensure that even large dogs cannot slip through or fall into the pool when the cover is closed.
What happens if someone walks on the cover?
A properly installed Integra cover will support the weight of an adult walking across it. However, pool covers are safety devices — not walking surfaces. The cover is designed to prevent drowning in accidental entry scenarios, not for intentional foot traffic.

Section 3B — Product Specs (6 Questions)

Product Specs

What material is the cover fabric?
Integra uses Aegis fabric — an 18-oz woven vinyl with an acrylic topcoat. The fabric is UV-stabilized, chemical-resistant, and engineered for the harsh pool environment including chlorine, bromine, salt, and ozone exposure.
How is the webbing attached?
All Integra covers use heat-sealed webbing — not stitching. Our Weldmaster process fuses the webbing directly to the fabric at a molecular level, eliminating needle holes and creating a bond stronger than the base material. This prevents the most common failure point in competitor covers.
What are the mechanisms made of?
100% stainless steel. Every guide rail, pulley, structural bracket, and load-bearing component is stainless steel. No aluminum, no galvanized steel, no mixed metals. This eliminates corrosion in chlorine and salt environments.
What colors are available?
Seven: Charcoal, Light Gray, Tan, Mocha, Royal Blue, Navy Blue, and Black. All colors use identical base fabric with the same UV stabilizers and chemical resistance.
Does color affect performance?
Slightly. Darker colors absorb more solar energy and can warm pool water by 2-5 degrees F more than lighter colors. Lighter colors stay cooler to the touch. All seven colors carry the same 7-year fabric warranty.
What mechanism types are available?
Integra offers two mechanism types: the Standard mechanism (top-track, ideal for new construction and retrofits) and the Low-Offset mechanism (recessed, for a flush deck appearance). Both use all-stainless steel construction and carry a 20-year warranty.

Section 3C — Installation (6 Questions)

Installation

Can an automatic cover be installed on an existing pool?
Yes. Integra's Standard (top-track) mechanism is specifically designed for retrofit installation on existing pools. The tracks mount on top of the pool coping, so there's no need to tear up your deck or modify the pool shell.
How long does installation take?
A typical residential installation takes 1-2 days. Retrofit installations (top-track) are usually completed in a single day. Recessed (low-offset) installations on new construction take 1-2 days depending on the housing and track configuration.
Do I need a specific pool shape?
Automatic covers work best on rectangular and geometric pools. Integra covers can accommodate most standard pool shapes. Free-form pools may require a modified approach — consult with your local Integra dealer for a site evaluation.
What size pools can be covered?
Integra covers are custom-manufactured to fit your pool's exact dimensions. We cover pools from small residential plunge pools up to large format pools. Maximum span widths depend on the mechanism type — your dealer will confirm sizing during the quote process.
Who installs the cover?
All Integra covers are installed by authorized, trained dealers. Every dealer goes through our installation certification program to ensure proper setup, safe operation, and full warranty coverage.
Will the installation damage my deck?
Top-track (retrofit) installations require minimal deck modification — the tracks mount to the existing coping with stainless steel hardware. Low-offset installations are typically planned during pool construction, so the housing is integrated into the deck design from the start.

Section 3D — Maintenance (5 Questions)

Maintenance

How do I clean the cover?
Rinse the fabric with a garden hose monthly to remove chlorine residue and debris. For a deeper clean, use mild soap and water twice a year. Never use a pressure washer, bleach, or abrasive cleaners — they damage the acrylic topcoat.
How often should I rinse the cover?
Monthly rinsing is recommended. In high-use seasons or salt pools, rinse every 2-3 weeks to prevent residue buildup.
Can the cover stay on during winter?
Yes. Integra covers are designed for year-round use. Maintain your water level and clear heavy snow loads promptly to prevent excess weight on the fabric.
Do the tracks need maintenance?
Flush the tracks with a garden hose quarterly to remove debris. Inspect for leaves, dirt, and small stones that could impede cover travel. Your dealer can perform an annual inspection as part of routine service.
How do I know when the fabric needs replacing?
Signs include visible fraying, persistent sagging even when tensioned, or cracking in the acrylic topcoat. Under normal conditions, Aegis fabric lasts well beyond the 7-year warranty. Contact your dealer for an assessment if you notice any of these signs.

Section 3E — Cost & Warranty (6 Questions)

Cost & Warranty

How much does an automatic pool cover cost?
Pricing varies based on pool size, mechanism type (top-track vs. recessed), and customization. A typical residential installation ranges from $10,000 to $18,000. Contact your local Integra dealer for a specific quote based on your pool dimensions.
How much will I save on heating?
By virtually eliminating evaporation — the primary source of heat loss — homeowners typically save 50-70% on pool heating costs. The cover traps solar heat during the day and prevents heat loss overnight.
How much will I save on chemicals?
Pool covers block UV rays (which degrade chlorine) and prevent debris contamination. Most homeowners save 35-60% on chemical costs annually.
What's the payback period?
Most homeowners see a full return on investment in 5-8 years through cumulative savings on water, energy, chemicals, and reduced cleaning time.
Does the cover extend my swim season?
Yes. By retaining daytime solar heat and preventing nighttime cooling, you can begin swimming 2-4 weeks earlier in spring and continue 2-4 weeks later in fall.
What does the warranty cover?
Integra offers a 20-year warranty on mechanisms (stainless steel components, motor, and structural elements) and a 7-year warranty on Aegis fabric. Warranty coverage requires installation by an authorized Integra dealer.
