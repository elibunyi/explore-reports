How an Automatic Pool Cover Works — From Mechanism to Motor

Behind the simple turn of a key or press of a button is a precision-engineered system designed for decades of reliable performance.

The Five Components of an Automatic Pool Cover System

Mechanism Drive Unit — 600x400px
01

The Mechanism (Drive Unit)

The powerhouse of the system. Our high-torque motor and drive shaft are built with all-stainless steel components for ultimate corrosion resistance.

  • check_circle Adjustable torque limiter for safety
  • check_circle Precision-aligned drum assembly
  • check_circle Industry-leading 20-year warranty
Rope & Pulleys — 600x400px
02

The Rope System and Pulleys

Engineered for silent, smooth travel. The system utilizes two high-tensile strength ropes guided by 2-inch precision pulleys.

Our oversized pulleys reduce friction significantly, extending the life of the ropes and minimizing mechanical strain on the drive unit.

Track Close-up — 600x400px
03

The Track System

The architectural guide for your cover. We offer multiple aluminum extrusion options to fit any pool design and aesthetic preference.

Standard Top Track Surface mounted for easy retrofitting.
Under-Track Concealed under the pool coping.
Aegis Fabric — 600x400px
04

The Fabric (Cover)

Our proprietary Aegis fabric is the barrier between your pool and the world. Engineered with heat-sealed webbing rather than traditional stitching for superior strength.

The Controls

Intelligence at your fingertips. Choose between high-tactile rocker switches or digital keypad controllers with customizable access codes.

Active-Hold Safety Feature: All Integra controls require continuous physical contact to operate, ensuring the operator is always present and attentive during movement.

What the Installation Looks Like

1

Site Assessment

Detailed measurements and elevation check of your pool deck.

2

Preparation

Cleaning the pool environment and clearing housing areas.

3

Track Installation

Precision alignment and anchoring of the track extrusions.

4

Mechanism Mounting

Securing the motor and stainless steel drive assembly.

5

Fabric Attachment

Installing the Aegis fabric and leader bars.

6

Rope Threading

Running the dual-rope system through the track and pulleys.

7

Control Wiring

Connecting electrical components and safety controls.

8

Test Operation

System calibration and final safety walk-through with homeowner.

bolt

Electrical Requirements

To ensure peak performance and safety, our systems require a dedicated 20A circuit with a GFCI breaker. Proper grounding is essential for motor longevity.

info Consult with your installer for local code compliance.
