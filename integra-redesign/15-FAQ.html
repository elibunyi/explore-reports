Safety & Certification

Are Integra covers ASTM certified? expand_more
Yes, every Integra cover meets or exceeds ASTM F1346 standards. This certification is the gold standard for manual and automatic pool safety covers.
What does ASTM F1346 require? expand_more
It mandates rigorous load tests (supporting 485 lbs static weight), strict perimeter gap limits to prevent child entry, and automated drainage protocols to prevent standing water accumulation on the cover.
Can a pool cover replace a fence? expand_more
In many jurisdictions, yes. A certified automatic safety cover is often recognized as a legal barrier equivalent to a fence. However, building codes vary. Check our state-by-state guide or consult your local building department for specific requirements.
How much weight can the cover hold? expand_more
Our covers are engineered and tested to support up to 485 lbs in a static load test, which is the capacity required for two adults and one child to be supported in an emergency scenario.
Is the cover safe for pets? expand_more
Absolutely. It is specifically designed to prevent accidental entry for both children and pets. The fabric tension and reinforced edges ensure that even large dogs cannot slip through the perimeter.

Maintenance & Care

Cleaning Frequency

We recommend rinsing the cover fabric every 2-3 months to remove salt and chlorine residue, and cleaning the tracks annually.

Winter Operation

Our covers can stay on during winter. Ensure the water level is maintained and heavy snow loads are cleared promptly.

Cost & Savings

How much does an automatic pool cover cost? expand_more
Pricing varies based on pool size, mechanism type (top-track vs. recessed), and customization options. A typical installation for a standard residential pool ranges between $10,000 and $18,000.
How much will I save on heating? expand_more
By virtually eliminating evaporation—the primary source of heat loss—homeowners typically save 50-70% on their pool heating costs.
How much will I save on chemicals? expand_more
By keeping UV rays (which degrade chlorine) and debris out of the water, you can save 35-60% on chemical consumption annually.
What's the payback period? expand_more
Most homeowners see a full return on their investment in 5 to 8 years through cumulative savings in water, energy, and chemicals.
Does the cover extend my swim season? expand_more
Yes. By retaining daytime solar heat and preventing nighttime cooling, you can often begin swimming 2-4 weeks earlier in the spring and continue 2-4 weeks later in the fall.
