Why 50% of Leads Are Unqualified

Three problems are working together to attract budget shoppers looking for $8K pools instead of $40K+ fiberglass installations. The conversion tracking issue is the root cause — it makes everything else harder to diagnose because the numbers look better than reality.

1. Conversion Tracking Is Inflated (Root Cause)

The account has 7 conversion actions ALL marked as primary. Google optimizes toward all of them equally — a brochure download counts the same as a phone call or quote request.

Conversion Action Category Quality Primary? Should Be Primary?
Phone Call Phone HIGH Yes Yes
Request Quote Form Submit HIGH Yes Yes
Free Estimate (web) Form Submit HIGH Yes Yes
Contact Form (web) Form Submit HIGH Yes Yes
Download Brochure Page View LOW Yes No
Download Brochure (web) Page View LOW Yes No
Contact Dealer (brand site) Page View LOW Yes No

The impact: A price-shopper Googling "how much does a pool cost" who clicks the ad and downloads a brochure registers as a "conversion." The CPA looks acceptable on paper because brochure downloads are cheap — but those people want an $8K pool and will never buy a $40K+ fiberglass installation.

Fix: Demote "Download Brochure," "Download Brochure (web)," and "Contact Dealer" to secondary (non-biddable). Only Phone Call, Request Quote, Contact Form, and Free Estimate should be primary conversion actions.

2. No Target CPA Cap

Both active campaigns run Maximize Conversions with target_cpa_micros = 0 — no cap. Google has no price signal telling it what a quality lead is worth. It maximizes volume, which means it bids on the cheapest conversions available (brochure downloads).

With the current bid strategy in learning mode after a recent change, this is the ideal time to set a target CPA before the system exits learning and locks into suboptimal patterns.

Fix: Set target CPA to $75–$100 after demoting brochure downloads. Start at $100 for 2 weeks, then tighten to $75 if lead quality improves. If volume drops too far, raise to $125 — but monitor lead quality, not just quantity.
